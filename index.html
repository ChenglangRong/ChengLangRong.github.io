Research Projects

Selected undergraduate research projects on reinforcement learning and intelligent scheduling for semiconductor manufacturing and central air-conditioning systems.

Project 01 2025.11-Present

Scheduling of Single-Arm Multicluster Tools for Multi-Type Wafers with Parallel Processing, Residency Time Constraints, and Reentrant Buffer Cooling Operations

CP-SAT Domain LNS GNN + RL Scheduling Single Robot Dual Cluster Tool Reentrant Buffer Cooling In Progress

Time

2025.11-Present

Role

Project Lead

Problem: Schedule multi-type wafers in a single-arm multicluster tool with reentrant buffer cooling.

Constraints: Parallel processing, residency-time limits, shared-resource exclusivity, and BM1 reentry and cooling logic.

Method: A three-layer framework combining CP-SAT, domain-specific LNS, and a GNN-guided RL controller.

Results: A unified exact model and learning-guided search pipeline are already running, with preliminary gains over a short-horizon exact baseline.

My Contribution: I lead the project and designed the modeling framework, core neighborhoods, and learning-guided optimization pipeline.

Project 02 2025.11-2026.03

Scheduling of Single-Arm Multicluster Tools for Multi-Type Wafers with Parallel Processing and Residency Time Constraints

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning Scheduling Single Robot Dual Cluster Tool SCI Q2 Submission in Preparation

Time

2025.11-2026.03

Role

Project Lead

Problem: Coordinate multi-type wafer scheduling in a multicluster tool with multiple interacting modules and robots.

Constraints: Shared BMs and PMs, residency-time limits, full-cycle scheduling, and deadlock-sensitive resource coupling.

Method: A multi-agent reinforcement-learning formulation with the MA-QMIX-PPO scheduling algorithm.

Results: The method achieved the best or tied-best minimum makespan against MAQMIX, MAPPO, MADDPG, MATD3, and MACTD4; the paper is being prepared for submission to an SCI Q2 journal.

My Contribution: I led the algorithm and experiment design for the project and am the second author on the journal manuscript.

Project 03 2025.06-2026.01

Scheduling of a Single-Arm Cluster Tool for Processing Two Types of Wafers with Parallel Processing

Deep Reinforcement Learning Scheduling Single Robot Single Cluster Tool CSCWD 2026 Accepted

Time

2025.06-2026.01

Role

Project Lead

Problem: Optimize full-cycle scheduling for a single-arm cluster tool processing two wafer types.

Constraints: Parallel PMs, shared modules, residency-time limits, and one-wafer-at-a-time robotic transport.

Method: An MDP formulation solved by an improved TD3 algorithm with dynamic exploration, prioritized replay, cyclic learning rate, and attention.

Results: The method delivered lower makespan, faster convergence, and stronger generalization than TD3, DDPG, and DQN; the first-author paper was accepted by CSCWD 2026, a CCF-C conference.

My Contribution: I completed the modeling, algorithm design, experiments, and paper writing as first author.

Project 04 2024.09-2025.07

Intelligent Scheduling System for Central Air Conditioning

Neural Networks DDPG HVAC Control Power Regulation Guangdong Regional Third Prize

Time

2024.09-2025.07

Role

Project Participant

Problem: Improve the coordinated scheduling of central air-conditioning equipment under changing operating conditions.

Constraints: Device coupling, external-condition limits, power-response uncertainty, and real-time control requirements.

Method: Neural-network-based power prediction combined with a DDPG scheduling and control system.

Results: The project produced an adaptive HVAC scheduling prototype and won Third Prize in the Guangdong Regional Competition of the China Robot and Artificial Intelligence Competition.

My Contribution: I participated in system analysis, predictive modeling, and reinforcement-learning-based scheduling implementation.
