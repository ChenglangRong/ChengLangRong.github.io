Project 01 2025.11-Present

Scheduling of Single-Arm Multicluster Tools for Multi-Type Wafers with Parallel Processing, Residency Time Constraints, and Reentrant Buffer Cooling Operations

CP-SAT Domain LNS GNN + RL Cooling Reentry RTSS 2026 Submission
Time2025.11-Present
RoleProject Lead

Problem: Schedule multi-type wafers in single-arm multicluster tools with explicit BM1 cooling reentry.

Method: A three-layer framework combining CP-SAT, domain-specific LNS, and a GNN-guided RL controller.

Progress: The exact model, key neighborhoods, and learning-guided destroy-repair loop are connected, with staged experimental gains under evaluation.

My Contribution: I lead the modeling framework, neighborhood design, optimization pipeline, and manuscript preparation.

First author-IEEE Real-Time Systems Symposium (RTSS 2026)-CCF-A conference

Project 02 2025.11-2026.03

Scheduling of Single-Arm Multicluster Tools for Multi-Type Wafers with Parallel Processing and Residency Time Constraints

Multi-Agent RL MA-QMIX-PPO Residency Constraints Benchmark Evaluation SCI Q1 Target CCF-B Journal
Time2025.11-2026.03
RoleProject Lead / First Author

Problem: Coordinate multiple robots and shared modules across multicluster semiconductor tools.

Method: A multi-agent reinforcement-learning formulation with centralized training, decentralized execution, action masking, value decomposition, and PPO policy stabilization.

Progress: Core algorithm design, scenario construction, and benchmark comparisons against MAQMIX, MAPPO, MADDPG, MATD3, and MACTD4 are complete.

My Contribution: I led algorithm design, experiment construction, performance analysis, and journal manuscript development.

First author-IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems-SCI Q1, CCF-B journal

Project 03 2025.06-2026.01

Scheduling of a Single-Arm Cluster Tool for Processing Two Types of Wafers with Parallel Processing

Deep RL Improved TD3 Cluster Tool CSCWD 2026 Accepted
Time2025.06-2026.01
RoleProject Lead / First Author

Problem: Optimize full-cycle scheduling for a single-arm cluster tool processing two wafer types.

Method: An MDP formulation solved by improved TD3 with dynamic exploration, prioritized replay, cyclic learning rate, attention, and normalized states.

Progress: Experiments show lower makespan, faster convergence, and stronger generalization than TD3, DDPG, and DQN.

My Contribution: I completed modeling, algorithm design, experiments, and paper writing.

First author-CSCWD 2026, a CCF-C conference-CCF-C conference
